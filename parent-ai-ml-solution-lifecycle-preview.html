AI/ML Solution Lifecycle

Review cadence: quarterly
Policy. The lifecycle described on this page applies to all AI/ML solutions at [Organisation]. Variations require explicit sign-off from [appropriate authority — to confirm].

Purpose

This page describes how AI/ML solutions move from idea to retirement at [Organisation]. It is the canonical reference for anyone delivering, governing, or supporting AI/ML work — from initial use case through to decommissioning. This parent page gives the whole lifecycle in about five minutes; the child pages provide the depth needed to operate within it.

It establishes a shared lifecycle, a common vocabulary, and a clear view of who is accountable at each stage. It does not replace existing policies on data, security, change management, or risk — it sits alongside them and shows where they apply within the lifecycle.

What counts as an AI/ML solution

In scope. Any in-house system that uses machine learning, deep learning, or generative AI to inform or automate decisions, generate content, or produce predictions used by people or downstream systems. This covers solutions built from scratch and those materially configured by the team, regardless of model class.

Out of scope. One-off exploratory notebooks and ad-hoc analyses that do not ship. Off-the-shelf SaaS features used without modification or material configuration. Procured or embedded third-party AI products are also out of scope at this stage; they will be brought into the lifecycle as the function matures.

The distinction between a model and a solution matters: a solution is a model plus its data pipeline, interfaces, monitoring, and accountable owner. This lifecycle governs solutions, not models in isolation.

Guiding principles

A small number of principles sit underneath the whole lifecycle. They are the reasons the gates and artefacts in the child pages exist, and they are the tie-breakers when this documentation does not have a specific answer.

  1. Outcome before sophistication. No solution proceeds without a clearly stated, measurable business outcome, and the simplest approach that meets it is preferred. Sophistication is earned by the problem, not assumed.
  2. Clear accountability at every stage. Each solution has one accountable team at each point in its life — Data Science through delivery, MLOps in operation — so it is always unambiguous who carries a given decision.
  3. Proportional, reversible delivery. The depth of governance scales with the impact of the solution, and solutions are built so they can be rolled back to a known-good state. Low-risk work moves quickly; high-stakes work is held to more.

The lifecycle at a glance

The lifecycle has six phases, from Intake through to Retirement. The first five carry a solution from idea to steady-state operation; Retirement is the terminal phase. A single feedback loop runs from Monitor back to Discovery: when a live solution needs a material change, it re-enters the lifecycle rather than being altered in place.

Phase ownership sits at department level: Data Science (DS) accountable for Intake through Productionise up to the handover gate, and MLOps accountable from that handover through Monitor and Retirement. Feasibility, though DS-led, is a cross-team check involving Data Solution, Data Engineering, MLOps, and AI Governance, with Platform collaborating on resources and environments. The detail of each phase is on The Lifecycle in Detail.
